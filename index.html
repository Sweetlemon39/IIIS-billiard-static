Operations and rules

Operations:

  • Mouse drag movement: rotate camera and cue;
  • "w": move forward;
  • "s": move backward;
  • "a": move left;
  • "d": move right;
  • "p": place the ball in free ball shooting mode;
  • "o": reset the player position and orientation;
  • "r": replay the last shot;
  • "h": hold or release the stick;
  • "j": make the sound louder;
  • "k": make the sound lower;
  • "q": cancel shooting when pressing space;
  • "/": focus to the chat input in multiplayer mode;
  • Space: the strength of shooting (shoot when release);
  • Shift: keep down (decrease the velocity at the same time);
  • ArrowUp: adjust up the shoot point;
  • ArrowDown: adjust down the shoot point;
  • ArrowLeft: adjust left the shoot point;
  • ArrowRight: adjust right the shoot point.

Rules:

The game is played with object balls ranging from 1 to 15 and a cue ball. If a player chooses to play from 1 to 7 (panchromatic) marked balls, the other player must play the number 9 to 15 (suit) target ball. The player first brings all the target balls of his own suit. After hitting the bag, hit the No. 8 ball into the bag to win the game.

The player will entry the free ball shooting mode when the following conditions are met:

  1. The cue ball goes into the pocket.
  2. The cue ball touches the illegal object ball.
  3. When the ball group has been determined, the player shot the wrong ball in other's group.
  4. A player hits the 8-ball. (The 8-ball is not in the pocket.)
  5. The player has not hit the ball.

The player will lose the game when the following conditions are met:

  1. Another player shots all the balls in his/her group as well as the No. 8 ball.
  2. The player shot in the last target ball in his group and No. 8 ball at the same time.
  3. When the player hasn't shot in all the target balls in his group but he shoots in No. 8 ball.
  4. No. 8 ball has been shot in and at the same time, the player has the foul action.

About

This is a project for the course "Advanced Computer Graphics" in IIIS, Tsinghua University by Ruijun Meng and Chengda Lu.

The main physical simulation references Leckie, W., & Greenspan, M. (2005). An event-based pool physics simulator.

We use three.js for graphics, math.js for mathematical calculation, and PeerJS for P2P online connections.

We referenced ekiefl/pooltool for UI and check of physical equations, and also jaks6/WebGL-Billiards for UI.

The assets are from sources listed below.

  • The model of the billiard table, cue and balls are from SketchFab "Billiard Table" by Futurealiti licensed under CC-BY-4.0. The model of the room is from SketchFab "The King's Hall" by Skokloster Castle (Skoklosters slott) licensed under Creative Commons Attribution.
  • The audio are from Soundsnap.
  • The game LOGO is generated by nulla2011/bluearchive-logo.
